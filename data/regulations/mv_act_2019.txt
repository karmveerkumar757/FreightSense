Motor Vehicles Act 2019 - Section 113 / 114 (Axle Load Limits & Overloading Regulations)
- Under the Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act 2019, overloading of vehicles attracts a severe penalty of Rs. 20,000 as a base fine, and an additional Rs. 2,000 per extra tonne of excess weight.
- Axle load limits are strictly governed according to the carrying capacity specified by the vehicle manufacturer and registered under the MV Act.
- Over-dimensional cargo (ODC) requires specific permissions and permits from the National Highways Authority of India (NHAI) and state transport authorities. If ODC cargo is transported without a valid ODC permit, it attracts Rs. 20,000 fine and the vehicle can be impounded.
- Strict height limits: Vehicles cannot exceed a height of 4.75 meters (15.5 feet) for double-deckers and 3.8 meters (12.5 feet) for standard goods carriers. Overheight trucks cannot pass through low flyovers and tunnels (e.g. bypass road barriers).
- Driver compliance: Drivers must carry a valid commercial driving license, fitness certificate, and pollution check (PUC) certificate. Violations attract Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 10,000 fines.
